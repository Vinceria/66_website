Our History

In 1888 Clarence Snowman acquired this land and built the current structure. He continued to work here until he sold the property to Charles F. Wescott in 1910.

Charles Wescottâ€™s blacksmith shop was a landmark in the village for fifty years. In his earlier years, he kept busy putting runners on sleds, rims on cars and carriages, shoes for horses and oxen, and making repairs on all sorts of ironwork. As the need for this type of work diminished, he turned to decorative forging, a trade in which he found himself to be very skillful. In his later years, he called his shop the Hammer & Tongs. His hardware, weather vanes, signs and other items of this sort made his work well known, and he continued to work at the forge until the time of his death in 1959.
